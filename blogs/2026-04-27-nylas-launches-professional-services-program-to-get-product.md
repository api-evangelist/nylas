<p><strong>What&#8217;s in this post:</strong> <em>How Nylas Professional Services helps product teams navigate Google OAuth verification, configure webhook architectures, and reach production faster — with structured onboarding programs, targeted à la carte sessions, and Proof of Concept engagements.</em></p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity" />



<p>Connecting to the Nylas API is straightforward. The documentation is clear, the SDKs are well-maintained, and most developers can get a working integration running in a matter of hours.</p>



<p>That&#8217;s not the hard part.</p>



<p>The hard part is what comes after: navigating Google&#8217;s OAuth verification process, configuring multi-environment authentication, designing a webhook architecture that holds up under real load, passing a Google Cloud Platform audit, and coordinating the handoffs between engineering, product, and go-to-market teams before you can actually ship. These are the steps that don&#8217;t show up in documentation, that coding assistants can&#8217;t walk you through, and that experienced engineers still get wrong on the first try.</p>



<p>This is the implementation gap — the distance between &#8220;integration built&#8221; and &#8220;integration in production&#8221; — and it&#8217;s where timelines slip, launches get delayed, and engineering cycles get spent on problems that have nothing to do with your product.</p>



<p>The Nylas Professional Services program is built to close that gap.</p>



<h2 id="three-onboarding-programs-scaled-to-your-deployment"><strong>Three onboarding programs, scaled to your deployment</strong></h2>



<p>Each onboarding program includes a dedicated Nylas engineer, a mutual action plan pre-populated with your success criteria, and a full documentation handoff at close. The dedicated engineer isn&#8217;t a support resource — they&#8217;re embedded in your process, helping you make architectural decisions early enough to matter.</p>



<p><strong>QuickLaunch (30 days)</strong> is built for teams that need to move fast. Two focused sprints cover architecture and authentication in weeks one and two, then logic, webhook setup, and verification prep in weeks three and four.</p>



<p><strong>QuickLaunch+ (60 days)</strong> goes deeper. Four sprints cover core architecture and authentication, data engineering and API optimization, real-time systems and webhook implementation, and verification and go-live prep — including a review of your provider verification demo videos before submission.</p>



<p><strong>Strategic Onboarding (90 days)</strong> is a concierge engagement for enterprise-grade integrations. Six sprints cover everything from architecture design and UI development to security hardening, beta testing support, and go-to-market strategy.</p>



<p>All three programs close with a full handoff: documentation, recorded calls, and API maps consolidated for your team&#8217;s long-term reference.</p>

<h2 id="targeted-help-for-specific-blockers"><strong>Targeted help for specific blockers</strong></h2>



<p>For teams that don&#8217;t need a full onboarding program but have a specific challenge to solve, Nylas offers à la carte sessions for the following services, with volume pricing available:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Google OAuth verification</strong> — a comprehensive review of your production environment and demo video to pass on the first attempt</li>



<li><strong>Provider OAuth app creation</strong> — collaborative setup of your Entra, GCP, and Zoom apps to avoid common misconfigurations</li>



<li><strong>Architecture and workflow review</strong> — practical feedback on your email and scheduling logic before it becomes a production problem</li>



<li><strong>Webhook and notification best practices</strong> — designing a reliable real-time data layer that handles retry logic, high concurrency, and system stability</li>



<li><strong>Go-to-market review</strong> — an audit of your user onboarding and sign-in flows to improve adoption from day one</li>
</ul>



<p>Each session runs as two 45-minute working sessions with a Nylas engineer, completed within 10 business days.</p>



<p><a href="https://www.nylas.com/contact-professional-services/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Reach out to learn more</a></p>



<h2 id="proof-of-concept-for-teams-that-need-validation-first"><strong>Proof of concept for teams that need validation first</strong></h2>



<p>The Proof of Concept engagement is for teams that need measurable evidence before committing to an annual or multi-year agreement. This is not a trial. It&#8217;s a structured engagement built around predefined, measurable KPIs aligned to your specific decision criteria.</p>



<p>The engagement runs in three phases: KPI definition and architecture alignment, controlled rollout and validation, and success measurement and review. Throughout, you receive structured technical advisory support and continuous performance monitoring. At the end, you have empirical data — the kind that supports internal budget approval — and a clear path forward into whichever agreement structure fits.</p>



<h2 id="why-this-matters-now"><strong>Why this matters now</strong></h2>



<p>Nylas processes more than 35 billion API transactions a month. That scale only matters if customers can build on it successfully.</p>



<p>The communications integrations teams are shipping today are more complex than they were two years ago. Products depend on communications data being accurate, normalized, and available in real time — feeding AI workflows, triggering automations, and surfacing structured context to users. The margin for integration error is smaller, and the cost of getting it wrong is higher.</p>



<p>At the same time, the tools available to developers have expanded significantly. Coding assistants can generate an integration. What they can&#8217;t do is walk you through Google OAuth verification, flag the webhook architecture decisions that break at scale, or help you absorb a breaking update from Microsoft Graph. That&#8217;s where expert human support picks up — and where the Professional Services program is designed to step in.</p>



<h2 id="get-started"><strong>Get started</strong></h2>



<p>The Nylas Professional Services program is available now for contract customers. Tell us where you are in your integration and we&#8217;ll match you to the right engagement.</p>
